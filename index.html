"The gap between what technology promises and what it delivers for the people it is meant to serve — that is where I work."

My work is not implementation. It is judgment. I engage with senior leaders and boards at the moments where the cost of misalignment exceeds the cost of delay — when structural risk is invisible, when vendor commitments are outpacing organizational readiness, or when transformation fatigue is eroding the capacity to execute.

I bring operating experience from tribal government, global manufacturing, and the United States Army Reserve — environments where decisions are irreversible, information is incomplete, and accountability is unambiguous.

How I Engage

Three Engagements.
One Standard.

I do not operate as an implementer or program manager. I partner with executive teams at inflection points where judgment, alignment, and risk management matter most.

Enterprise Technology Operating Model Review

  • Assess how technology actually operates versus how strategy assumes it does
  • Determine whether decision rights, structure, and incentives are enabling progress or quietly increasing exposure
  • Deliver a concise executive brief — structural risks, leverage points, and required leadership actions

Executive Decision Support for Large-Scale Change

  • Pressure-test assumptions before commitments become irreversible
  • Surface second-order impacts across platform strategy, sourcing, and organizational design
  • Strengthen decision quality without introducing additional delivery complexity

Board-Level Technology and Risk Readiness

  • Translate technical realities into governance narratives boards can act on
  • Help boards ask the right questions — and executives answer with credibility
  • Build durable oversight capacity, not compliance theater

If you are navigating complexity at the enterprise or board level and value clear judgment grounded in operating experience, I welcome a conversation.
