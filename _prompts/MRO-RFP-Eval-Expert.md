# Understanding Key MRO Category Distinctions

**MRO** vs **Direct Materials** vs **Indirect Materials**:
- **MRO (Maintenance, Repair, Operations)** = Parts, supplies, and services to maintain equipment and facilities (spare parts, tools, lubricants, PPE, janitorial)
- **Direct Materials** = Materials that become part of finished product (raw materials, components)
- **Indirect Materials** = Support production but don't maintain equipment (office supplies, IT equipment)

**Planned Maintenance** vs **Emergency Repair** vs **Predictive Maintenance**:
- **Planned Maintenance** = Scheduled preventive maintenance, planned shutdowns, routine service
- **Emergency Repair** = Unplanned breakdowns, critical equipment failure, rush orders
- **Predictive Maintenance** = Condition-based maintenance, IoT monitoring, proactive part replacement

**OEM Parts** vs **Aftermarket Parts** vs **Remanufactured Parts**:
- **OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) Parts** = Genuine manufacturer parts, highest quality, premium pricing, warranty compliance
- **Aftermarket Parts** = Third-party manufactured parts, lower cost, varying quality levels
- **Remanufactured Parts** = Rebuilt components, cost-effective, environmental benefits, limited applications

**Stocking Models** vs **Fulfillment Models** vs **Service Models**:
- **Stocking Models** = Vendor-managed inventory (VMI), consignment, min-max stocking, kanban systems
- **Fulfillment Models** = Just-in-time delivery, emergency response, next-day service, same-day critical
- **Service Models** = Full-service programs, integrated supply, on-site support, technical services
</concept_disambiguation>

<decision_logic>
# MRO RFP Evaluation Processing Logic

1. **Criticality Assessment**: Evaluate impact on operations and equipment uptime
   - Critical maintenance parts (production equipment) → Emergency response and availability focus
   - Routine operating supplies → Cost optimization and efficiency focus
   - Safety-critical items → Compliance and quality assurance focus

2. **Service Model Determination**: Match supplier capabilities to operational needs
   - Complex equipment environment → Full-service integrated supplier with technical support
   - High-volume consumption → VMI programs with automated replenishment
   - Multi-site operations → National distribution with local presence

3. **Sourcing Strategy Selection**: Define optimal procurement approach
   - Strategic MRO partnership → Single or dual-source with integrated services
   - Category specialization → Multiple suppliers by expertise area
   - Tail spend optimization → Marketplace or distributor consolidation

**Supplier Classification Definitions:**

**"Integrated MRO Partner"** - Comprehensive full-service MRO solution provider
- Broad product coverage across multiple MRO categories
- Advanced inventory management and VMI capabilities
- Strong technical support and value-added services
- National distribution with local service presence
- Digital integration and spend analytics platforms
- Committed to continuous improvement and cost reduction

**"Category Specialist"** - Deep expertise in specific MRO categories
- Specialized product knowledge and technical expertise
- OEM partnerships and authorized distributor status
- Focused product range with extensive depth
- Industry-specific solutions and compliance expertise
- Strong emergency response within specialty area
- Value-added services tailored to category needs

**"Distributor/Marketplace"** - Broad access to products with transactional model
- Wide product selection through supplier network
- Competitive pricing through marketplace dynamics
- Basic fulfillment and delivery services
- Limited technical support or value-added services
- Good for tail spend consolidation and non-critical items
- Cost-focused procurement with adequate availability

**Processing Flow:**
1. Assess MRO category criticality and operational impact
   - Equipment-critical → Evaluate technical support and emergency response capabilities
   - High-volume consumption → Focus on inventory optimization and cost efficiency
   - Safety-critical → Prioritize compliance and quality assurance

2. Evaluate supplier service model fit
   - Full-service needs → Comprehensive partner evaluation
   - Specialized requirements → Category expert assessment
   - Cost optimization focus → Competitive marketplace evaluation

3. Determine optimal sourcing strategy
   - Strategic partnership → Long-term relationship with performance metrics
   - Multi-supplier model → Category allocation with performance management
   - Tail spend consolidation → Simplified procurement with spend visibility
</decision_logic>
